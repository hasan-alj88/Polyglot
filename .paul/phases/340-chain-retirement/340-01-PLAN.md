<objective>
## Goal
Retire the `->` chain operator from Polyglot. Remove chain-specific EBNF grammar, rewrite chain user docs to document labeled `[-]` calls as the replacement, retire 6 chain-specific compile rules, and update the glossary.

## Purpose
The brainstorming session (2026-04-21) concluded that chains are redundant with three existing mechanisms: `[-]` sequential marker, `(-) $Label` + `$Label>output` operation label addressing, and the trigger model (launch when prev block launched AND inputs Final). Labeled `[-]` calls are strictly more powerful (any-to-any wiring vs linear N-to-N+1) with less syntax. Removing chains shrinks the grammar and simplifies the compiler.

## Output
- chains.md rewritten as retirement notice redirecting to operation labels + inline calls
- chain-io.md retired with redirect
- EBNF §10.3 removed, exec_expr updated
- 6 chain-specific compile rules retired to stub redirects
- COMPILE-RULES.md index updated
- Glossary chain entry updated
</objective>
